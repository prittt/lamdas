Call for papers

TL;DR The workshop will cover anything using, implementing, improving binary image analysis, a specific area of image processing, which is less mainstream nowadays, but which still supports most of the computer vision systems implementations.

Deep Learning, and Convolutional Neural Networks in general, whose growth in popularity began in the early 2010s, have marked a shift of Computer Vision, permeating most of the academic research fields of the last decade. Therefore, important questions come to mind: «Is computer vision and binary image processing without machine learning still worth it?», «How significant is the improvement of these kinds of algorithms, both in terms of performance and accuracy, in the deep learning era?»

As a matter of fact, most of the state-of-the-art solutions exploit binary image processing algorithms as fundamental pre- or post-processing steps to get to the final results or to prepare training data.

When segmenting images, a highly relevant task in medical imaging, Connected Components Labeling is usually exploited together with voting strategies to remove noise and produce the final segmentation map or to count objects. Thinning, instead, is often used together with contour-tracing and morphological operators, whenever a compact representation of the objects inside an image is required, as in fingerprint analysis, vasculature geometry detection, and road mapping. Therefore, also deep learning pipelines can benefit from efficient implementations of binary image processing algorithms.

Moreover, given that image processing algorithms represent the base step of many real-time applications, they are required to be as fast as possible. Thus, research in the field moved towards optimizing the performance of these algorithms, i.e., the execution speed.

This workshop aims at collecting novel proposals which employ binary (two level) images. These may be:

  • algorithmic improvements to speed up existing techniques in CPU, GPU and/or FPGA.
  • novel approaches or features/descriptors which use binary images as intermediate steps to save memory or to allow their use in embedded environments
  • techniques which employ binary maps for neural networks, used for image analysis
  • specific approaches for image binarization, based on any segmentation criterion
  • algorithms which produce binary output maps
  • connected components labeling and analysis, mathematical morphology, skeletonization, contour detection and tracking, binarization techniques
  • extension of existing algorithms to 3D volumes and/or multidimensional data.
